Ring 0 · Your front door

01Orientation

You've landed about as well as anyone could. Your flat on Bitter End sits inside Brewery Square — the old Eldridge Pope brewery, now Dorchester's eating, drinking and cinema quarter — and directly opposite Dorchester South station. Most of your daily life is a two-to-ten-minute walk from the front door, the county's coast is a short hop south, and the whole of the South West opens up from there. This guide is built as rings radiating out from that door: your doorstep, then the town, then the county, then the region, then abroad.

Your neighbourhood — home (amber), the two stations, the hospital, and the town-centre places. Tap a marker for its name.

02Practical setup & admin

Council tax & bins

Dorset Council is your unitary authority. Register for council tax as soon as you move in (a 25% single-occupant discount applies if you'll be the only adult). Bins run on Dorset's standard rhythm: weekly food waste, fortnightly recycling, fortnightly rubbish, with garden waste as a paid add-on. Use the council's “My local information” address lookup for your exact days — collection days across southwest Dorset were reshuffled through 2025/26, so don't assume.

Utilities & broadband

Your water/sewerage is Wessex Water (regional, no choice). Electricity and gas you can switch to any supplier — a Brewery Square flat will already be metered. For broadband, check your exact postcode before committing: the development generally has good fibre, but confirm whether full-fibre (FTTP) is live at Alexandra House rather than assuming.

03Getting around

To the hospital

Dorset County Hospital is on Williams Avenue — a 15–20 min walk, ~5 min drive, or a very easy 5–7 min cycle. Staff parking is limited and usually permit/ballot-based, so walking or cycling sidesteps the whole headache.

Dorset County Hospital ↗

Your workplace. Williams Avenue, northwest of the centre.

~15–20 min walk

Trains

Two stations. Dorchester South (opposite you) — South Western Railway to London Waterloo, and south to Weymouth in ~11 min. Dorchester West (~10 min walk) — GWR's Heart of Wessex line up to Yeovil, Castle Cary, Bath and Bristol. Last trains run early-ish, which matters for nights out.

Dorchester South station ↗

Your doorstep station: London Waterloo + Weymouth line.

~2 min walk

Buses

Local services run from the town-centre bus station. The one worth knowing is the X51/X53 Jurassic Coaster, which threads the coast (Axminster–Weymouth–Poole) — a cheap, car-free way to reach the coastal towns.

Driving, parking & charging

Town car parks are pay-and-display (Acland Rd under Waitrose, Charles St, Top o' Town); your flat should have allocated parking. Petrol is easiest at the Tesco on Weymouth Ave. EV chargers are in Brewery Square and the council car parks.

Night out / clubs — the honest version

Dorchester has essentially no nightclubs — it's a pubs-and-cocktails town that quietens after ~11pm midweek. For an actual club night you go to Weymouth (train ~11 min, drive ~20–25 min; the last train back is early, so budget for a taxi). For a proper big one, Bournemouth.

10International travel

Four regional airports and three ferry ports, all closer than London.

Airports

Bournemouth Airport (BOH) ↗

Your closest by far (~30 mi, drive ~50–min–1h). A holiday airport — ~42 destinations on Ryanair, TUI and Jet2 covering Spain, the Canaries, Greek islands, Portugal, Cyprus, Malta, Morocco and a few European cities. No rail link; drive or taxi.

~50 min–1h drive

Exeter Airport (EXT) ↗

~55 mi, drive ~1h10–20. The one that opens up the world: KLM flies daily to Amsterdam (160+ onward connections), plus Aer Lingus to Dublin (US pre-clearance), Loganair to Manchester/Edinburgh, and TUI holiday routes. Your smartest option for a real international trip.

drive ~1h10–20

Southampton Airport (SOU) ↗

~55 mi, drive ~1h — but the no-car star: a direct train from Dorchester South runs hourly in ~1h35, and the station is under a minute from the terminal. Aer Lingus (Dublin), Loganair (Scotland), Aurigny (Channel Islands) plus seasonal holiday flights.

direct train ~1h35

Bristol Airport (BRS) ↗

~75 mi, drive ~1h30–50 — the furthest but by far the widest network: easyJet's base, plus Ryanair, TUI and Jet2 across most of Europe and the bigger city destinations. No direct rail; drive.

drive ~1h30–50

Ferry ports

Because you’re near the coast, taking the car to France or Spain by sea is a genuinely good option — you arrive with your own wheels and skip airports entirely.

Poole ↗

Your closest port (~28 mi, ~40–50 min). Channel Islands (Guernsey) and, currently, Cherbourg — though from ~Nov 2026 the Cherbourg crossing shifts to Portsmouth, with Poole keeping the fast craft to Guernsey.

~40–50 min drive

Portsmouth ↗

The big one for the Continent (~75 mi, ~1h30–45): Brittany Ferries to Caen, Cherbourg and St Malo in France, and overnight mini-cruises to Bilbao and Santander in northern Spain (20–32h), skipping the drive across France.

~1h30–45 drive

Plymouth ↗

A western alternative (~55 mi, ~1h15): Roscoff in Brittany and a Santander service to Spain.

~1h15 drive
